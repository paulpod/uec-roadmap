Urgent & Emergency care: roadmap for digital health and care services

From the Digital Transformation Portfolio

About this roadmap

The Five Year Forward View sets out that to meet rising demand, the NHS must harness the power of information and technology to provide care and services that are convenient for patients, efficient for the NHS and which get people the right care for them as quickly as possible

This roadmap sets out our plans to provide the infrastructure, systems, technology and processes that enable the development of digital urgent care services. We will improve telephone and online triage and provide improved technology to support clinicians, so that treatment is better targeted and patients are referred to the right place, first time

For more information visit https://www.england.nhs.uk/urgent-emergency-care/

Available now

NHS 111 Online live nationally

NHS 111 online is being made available across the country this year, connecting patients to local services for help when they need it

http://111.nhs.uk

NHS 111 can book Out of Hours GP appointments

Appointments can now be booked from NHS 111 into the majority of GP Out of Hours services and into many Urgent Treatment Centre services

Electronic Prescriptions

Supplier approval to route electronic prescriptions from NHS 111 and GP Out of Hours to pharmacies via EPS has begun

https://digital.nhs.uk/services/…

Pharmacy profile updates

Profile Updater is live in BETA enabling all pharmacy providers to keep their service information (e.g. opening times) up to date on the Directory of Services (DoS)

https://digital.nhs.uk/services/directory-of-services-dos

Access to Mental Health and End of Life records

Clinicians in over 50% of urgent treatment centres and Emergency Departments have electronic or manual access to patients’ Mental Health Records and End of Life records

NHS Pathways Repeat Prescription Module

Enable specific high volume Repeat Prescription calls to be managed by users trained in this module only. This increases availability of call handlers and clinicians in IUC, fast tracks appropriate calls to clinicians and also reduces provider training commitments

Summary Care Records additional info

Clinicians have access to Summary Care Records (SCR) for 15% of patients that have additional information in their SCR

https://digital.nhs.uk/services/summary-care-records-scr

Local services prioritised in DoS

Commissioners can prioritise services that are locally-commissioned to ensure patients are directed to the most appropriate services, and ensure they aren’t directed to services that aren’t able to help them

https://digital.nhs.uk/services/directory-of-services-dos

Ambulance strategy

Digital strategy available now to inform and support the digitisation of information made available to Ambulance services

Standards and specifications

Publication of initial standards and specifications that will enable suppliers to develop products that integrate into the current UEC systems

NHS Pathways updated to support triage

Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS) to support non-clinical and clinical triage in 111/999/OOH. This update contains a range of content changes including; revised content for patients who have fallen, further sepsis content, support for 999 services with a major incident feature and updates for IUC CAS products

Waiting Times - private beta

Live wait times from Emergency Departments and Urgent Treatment Centres to be made available to NHS 111 Health Advisors and Clinician Advisors, helping them provide patients with the most efficient and effective service


Oct - Dec 2018

Service Finder tool available for clinicians (Private Beta)

A mobile responsive web application will be used by frontline Urgent and Emergency Care professionals to find appropriate services for their patients. It will include search methods to support the different ways in which professionals try to locate those services

NHS Pathways Dental Module

Dental Module enables specific high volume dental calls to be managed by users trained in this module only. This increases availability of call handlers and clinicians in IUC and also reduces provider training commitments

NHS Pathways Senior Clinician Module v3

New version available to SCM users with streamlined workflow, improved routing for chest pain, possible stroke and allergic reaction symptoms plus expanded Pharmacy and Mental Health options

UEC Data and Information Strategy 

The development of the UEC data strategy will articulate the future direction of data within UEC. This will help to drive evidence-based decision making and to share data, insight and intelligence more widely

111 Service update

Changes made to the 111 Service following enhanced data analysis


January - March 2019

Support function for suppliers

Establish a function that will support new and existing suppliers using UEC standards, providing them with all necessary documentation, specifications and processes to enable them to gain the necessary assurance and certification to utilise the standards

https://developer.nhs.uk/library/…

NHS 111 online can validate 999 and ED triage outcomes

Users can speak to a clinician when their triage suggests they might need to ring 999 for a low-acuity ambulance or go to an emergency treatment centre

Publication of improved interoperability guidance on developer.nhs.uk

Further information at https://developer.nhs.uk/apis/cds-api/index.html from Q4 18/19

Ambulance telephone call analysis

Discovery and alpha on application of natural language processing on nature of call and call audit


April - June 2019

Send NHS 111 online cases to pharmacists

Users with minor illnesses can have their case sent electronically to a pharmacy where they can receive advice and medication as part of the DMIRS scheme

NHS Pathways Release 17 to support triage

Beta testing and commencement of national roll-out of R17. Clinical improvements to choking, drowning and cold exposure pathways and for unexpected deaths, non-mobile babies and ingested objects


July 19 - 2020

NHS 111 online can book into urgent care services

Users who need to visit an urgent care service, like an out-of-hours GP, can book their appointment online

Connect NHS 111 online to online GP consultations

Can users who need to speak to a GP today be linked directly into their local online consultation system?

New search APIs for Directory of Services

Allowing system suppliers to perform more types of search against the DoS from within their applications

NHS 111 can book UTC appointments

Working with a range of existing UEC suppliers to develop standardised appointment booking solutions. NHS 111 professionals will be able to directly book patient appointments into Urgent Treatment Centres

NHS 111 can book in-hours GP appointments

Working with a range of existing UEC suppliers to develop standardised appointment booking solutions. NHS 111 professionals will be able to directly book patient appointments into In Hours GPs

http://111.nhs.uk

Supplier framework

Framework to enable providers and commissioners to understand what approved Clinical Decision Support Systems are available

Live Wait Times available to NHS 111 (Public Beta)

Enabling live wait times from Emergency Departments and Urgent Treatment Centres to be made available to 111 Health Advisors and Clinical Advisors, helping them provide patients with the most efficient and effective service

All appointments within UEC booked electronically

Clinicians in all NHS 111 and Urgent Treatment Centre providers will be able to book appointments directly into Urgent Treatment Centres, GPs, and Emergency Dental services

All UEC services will have access to DoS

Through a combination of a mobile responsive application and DoS APIs, UEC Professionals, Ambulance Trusts and Community Pharmacies will be able to gain access to the Directory of Service when they need it
